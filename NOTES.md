# Notes

Various design notes for the Unsafe Engine mechanics.

## Mechanics' Bucket List

- OSR-style for all system elements and features.
- Classic 6 ability scores (STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA).
  - 3d6 roll-high to generate abilities; 3-18 with 10 being ordinary.
  - ADV/DIS for specific origins and/or callings.
  - Extreme scores of 1-2 are feeble, 10 are ordinary, and 19+ are extraordinary.
- Player-facing checks: All rolls are made by the players in the form of action or reaction checks. The GM rarely rolls dice, allowing more focus on the narrative.
- Checks are performance-based single rolls; a 3d6 roll-under dice mechanic for all checks. There are modifiers to dice rolls; what you roll is what you get (WYRIWYG).
- Target numbers (TN) are based on appropriate ability scores per the action. An ability score of 10 (ordinary) grants a base 50% to accomplish an action.
- Challenge ratings (CR) modify the target numbers; the higher the CR, the more challenging the action.
  - For task checks (non-combat actions), the CR is determined by the GM, per the Challenge Scale (see below).
  - For action and reaction checks (conflicting actions), the CR is determined using the CR value of the monster in question, further modified by the GM (if the situation warrants a modifier).
  - For contested action (PC vs. PC): Opposing task or action/reaction checks with an obvious winner or loser; the margin determines performance.
- Whether the roll is an action or reaction check, the performance is determined by the margin of success versus the target number. In combat, base damage is determined by the performance modifier (PM) of the PC's weapon or the monster's attack, adjusted by the performance of the attack itself, below the TN or above the TN. If 0 damage is reached, then the attack failed.
